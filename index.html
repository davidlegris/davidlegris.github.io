About Me

David Legris is a GIS professional living in Burnaby, British Columbia with an interst in data management and application development. Previously, David worked with the City of Winnipeg Insect Control Branch, before moving to Thailand, where he taught English for 5 years. Having returned, David now enjoys writing with GoGeomatics Canada, and has recently been spending his time improving his JavaScript skills.

Education

David studied Environmental Science at the University of Manitoba, where he focused on topics related to sustainable development. During his studies, David completed an honours thesis research project comparing mosquito characteristics, neighbourhood composition, land cover and public opinion in relation to the City of Winnipeg's adult mosquito control program. The purpose of this project was to better understand the factors driving treatment decisions.


Later, David completed the GIS Advanced Diploma Program with BCIT, providing a strong foundation in GIS principles and methodology. During this program, David developed skills including spatial analysis, data management, application design, automation and other topics related to GIS software, data and infrastructure. David completed a practicum placement with United Way Centraide Canada, where he created a Leaflet-based web map application for internal data visualization, along with automation scripts and user documentation.
